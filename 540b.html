About.

This is a second semester graduate level course in algebraic topology. We will cover various advanced topics, sampling many of the highlights from the 20th century while building the foundations for modern research in topology.

Possible topics.

  • Poincaré duality
  • generalized cohomology theories
  • stable homotopy theory
  • vector bundles
  • principal bundles and their classifying spaces
  • Eilenberg-MacLane spaces
  • equivariant cohomology
  • characteristic classes
  • cohomology operations
  • topological K theory
  • Bott periodicity
  • spectral sequences
  • cobordism theory

Textbooks and references.

  • Algebraic Topology - Hatcher [H1]
  • Characteristic Classes - Milnor and Stasheff [MS] (see also here for modern typesetting)
  • Vector Bundles and K-Theory - Hatcher [H2]
  • K-Theory - Atiyah [A]
  • Cohomology Operations and Applications in Homotopy Theory - Mosher and Tangora [MT]
  • A User's Guide to Spectral Sequences - McCleary [Mc]
  • An Introduction to Homological Algebra - Weibel [W]
  • Spectral Sequences in Algebraic Topology - Hatcher [H3]
  • You Could Have Invented Spectral Sequences - Chow [C]
